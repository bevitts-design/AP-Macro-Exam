Global Balance & FOREX

Mastering the Balance of Payments, Foreign Exchange Markets, and International Capital Flows.

Balance of Payments (BOP)

[cite_start]The BOP records all international transactions between a country and the rest of the world[cite: 3502]. [cite_start]Rule: It must always sum to zero (CA + CFA = 0)[cite: 3503].

Current Account (CA)

[cite_start]Tracks the trade in goods and services (Net Exports), net investment income, and net transfers[cite: 3504].

Capital & Financial Account (CFA)

[cite_start]Tracks the purchase and sale of financial assets (such as stocks and bonds) and physical capital[cite: 3505].

[cite_start]The Balancing Act: A deficit in the Current Account is always offset by a surplus in the Capital and Financial Account[cite: 3506].

Shifting the FOREX Market

[cite_start]

When demand increases or supply decreases, the currency appreciates[cite: 3510]. [cite_start]When demand decreases or supply increases, it depreciates[cite: 3511].

Real Interest Rates

[cite_start]

If a country's real interest rate rises, it attracts foreign investors seeking higher returns[cite: 3515]. [cite_start]This increases demand for the currency (Appreciation)[cite: 3517].

Relative Incomes

[cite_start]

If a country has higher relative income growth, consumers demand more imports[cite: 3520]. [cite_start]This increases the supply of the domestic currency (Depreciation)[cite: 3521].

Relative Inflation

[cite_start]

If a country has lower relative inflation, its goods become cheaper[cite: 3522]. [cite_start]This increases demand for its currency to buy those goods (Appreciation)[cite: 3523].

AP FRQ Favorite

The Ultimate Chain of Reasoning

[cite_start]

AP Free-Response Questions heavily test your ability to trace the step-by-step impact of domestic changes on the international market[cite: 3514].

Step 1: Interest
[cite_start]

Real interest rate rises (e.g., due to deficit borrowing)[cite: 3515].

Step 2: Capital Flows
[cite_start]

Attracts foreign investors, creating financial capital inflows (CFA surplus)[cite: 3515, 3516].

Step 3: FOREX
[cite_start]

Demand for the domestic currency increases, causing it to Appreciate[cite: 3517].

Step 4: Net Exports

Exports become expensive, imports become cheaper. [cite_start]Net Exports decrease[cite: 3518, 3519].

Key Takeaways

  • Appreciation = Net Exports
  • Depreciation = Net Exports
  • CA Deficit = Financed by CFA Surplus.
