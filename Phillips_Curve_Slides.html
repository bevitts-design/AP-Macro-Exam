The Phillips Curve

Mastering Inflation, Unemployment, and the graphs that will score you a 5 on the AP Exam.

The Basics

The Phillips Curve is a foundational macroeconomic model that shows the relationship between the Inflation Rate and the Unemployment Rate.

Graphing Non-Negotiables:

  • Y-Axis: Inflation Rate (NOT Price Level!)
  • X-Axis: Unemployment Rate (NOT Real GDP!)

Short-Run (SRPC)

The Short-Run Phillips Curve (SRPC) slopes downward.

It demonstrates an inverse trade-off between inflation and unemployment in the short run.

  • High Inflation = Low Unemployment
  • Low Inflation = High Unemployment
Crucial Exam Rule

Movements vs. Shifts

Changes in Aggregate Demand (AD) cause a movement along the SRPC.

AD Increases

Unemployment falls, inflation rises.
→ Movement UP & LEFT on SRPC.

AD Decreases

Unemployment rises, inflation falls.
→ Movement DOWN & RIGHT on SRPC.

Shifting the SRPC

The entire SRPC shifts due to two primary factors:

1. Changes in SRAS

AS shifts cause SRPC to shift in the opposite direction.
Example: Negative supply shock (Stagflation) = SRAS ↓ = SRPC Shifts RIGHT.

2. Inflationary Expectations

If people expect higher inflation, SRPC shifts UP/RIGHT.

Long-Run (LRPC)

The Long-Run Phillips Curve (LRPC) is a vertical line.

  • Anchored at NRU: It sits precisely at the Natural Rate of Unemployment (Full-Employment).
  • No Trade-off: Proves that in the long run, there is NO trade-off between inflation and unemployment.
  • The Intersection: Where SRPC and LRPC cross = The Expected Rate of Inflation.
AP FRQ Favorite

The Long-Run Adjustment

1

Short-Run Shock

Govt uses expansionary policy. AD increases. Economy moves UP and LEFT along the SRPC (lower unemployment, higher inflation).

2

Expectations Adjust

Over time, workers and businesses adjust their expectations to the new, higher inflation rate. Input costs (wages) begin to rise.

3

Long-Run Shift

SRAS decreases, causing the entire SRPC to shift UP/RIGHT. Unemployment returns to NRU, but inflation is permanently higher.

Exam Cheat Sheet

The Rules

  • Δ in AD = Movement ALONG SRPC
  • Δ in AS = Shift of SRPC (Opposite direction)
  • Δ in Expected Infl. = Shift of SRPC

FRQ Pitfalls

  • Label axes exactly: Inflation Rate & Unemployment Rate.
  • If given numbers (e.g., 5% NRU), plot them on the graph!
  • Explain the Chain of Reasoning (Policy → AD → Phillips Curve).
